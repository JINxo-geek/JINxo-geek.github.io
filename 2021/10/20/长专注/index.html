Abstaining behaviors can be really powerful for training the brain but I think they also 避开/戒掉某些行为 对于训练大脑而言 真正有效 不过我认为

have to be met with some skepticism. 不可避免的也会遇到一些疑虑

Let’s assume that doing an activity will not give me any pleasure for 80 years; I start 我们假设进行一项活动80年 此其间不能带来任何快乐

doing it at the age of 10.But, when I hit 90 years of age, this activity will produce 比如说我从10岁开始进行吧 当到90岁时 这项活动将产生出

extreme amounts of net pleasure in my life. 我的一生中 最为巨量的纯粹快乐

There is a big problem if I die before that. 但如果我在那之前就死了 这就是个大问题

While trying to maximize my pleasure, I died with an absolute minimum. 在试图达到最大化快乐的期间 我死了 就绝对只带着最小量快乐死了

I call this the abstaining problem. 我称之为 戒断难题

To avoid dying with a minimum of pleasure, it’s important to inject a controlled amount 为了避免死时 仅有过最小量快乐 适当添加一定数量可控的

of short-term pleasures. 短期愉悦很重要的

Habit #2: Controlled Injection of Pleasure - Do Before You Die 这就来到习惯2 可控愉悦的添加 死之前做吧

One of the most powerful ways to inject controlled pleasure into your life is to separate it 在生活中添加可控愉悦的 最有用方式之一

into periods where you pursue long term pleasures and periods where you pursue short term pleasures. 就是将追寻长期愉悦与寻求短期愉悦 分成不同时间段

Here are some practices that can help with that. 这里有一些练习可帮助你做到

Carefully select times in the day when you will check social media, watch TV, or play 练习一 媒体控制 仔细选择一天中 上社交媒体 看电视 或玩游戏

video games. 的时间

Choose what devices they will be on too. 选择何种设备来用 也要考虑在内

Maybe you only check social media while you’re on your desktop and you only play video games 也许你只在桌面电脑上查看社交媒体

on the living room TV which you have limited access to. 只在客厅电视上的有限制接口/通路上玩游戏

Be deliberate about when you engage in these short-term pleasures. 要深思熟虑 何时进行这些短期愉悦活动

Dictate when you use them; don’t let them dictate you. 自己决定何时使用 而不是让它们来支配你

If you want to learn how I control social media you can watch my video on it which I’ve 如果你想看看 我是如何控制社交媒体的视频

linked to in the description. 描述栏中有附链接 练习二 规定结束时间

Imagine these two scenarios: I tell you to run hard for 200m or I just tell you to run 想想下列两种情况 我要你尽全力跑200米 或者 仅仅是要你尽全力跑

hard.

Your time would likely be faster in the former case because you know how long you’ll have 在前一种情况下 你的时间可能会过得更快一点 因为你知道需要尽全力的

to expend yourself for. 时间是多长

In the latter example, you will likely preserve your energy because your don’t know when 在后一种情况 你很可能会保留精力 因为不知道什么时候

I will tell you to stop. 我才会叫你停下来

Likewise, it’s a lot easier to direct focus when we know the bounds of when to start and 同样 当知道何时开始与何时结束的界限时 更加容易控制

when to end. 我们的注意力

Set a quitting time for each day, after which you will do no more work. 每天都设定一个结束时间 在那之后不做任何任务

After the quitting time, there are no limits on your short term pleasures. 在结束时间之后 寻求短期愉悦活动将无任何限制

Before the quitting time, you have to limit them. 而在结束时间之前 你必须限制它们 练习三 建立工作区块

A good way to balance short-term and long-term pleasures before your quitting time is to 在结束时间之前 有一个好方法 用来平衡短期和长期愉悦活动

structure periods of intense focus followed by periods of breaks. 在极专注时间段后 紧接着安排休息时间

A common technique for this is the pomodoro technique. 一个常见技巧 就是番茄工作法

Typically, the pomodoro technique entails someone working for 25 minutes followed by 一般而言 番茄工作法 要求进行25分钟工作

a 5 minute break. 之后马上休息5分钟

After 4 25 minute working sessions, they take a longer break of 30 minutes. 进行4个25分钟工作段后 进行一个长些的30分钟休息

In those break times, they can indulge in whatever short term pleasure they want. 在休息时间 可以做任何想做的短期愉悦活动

During the periods of focus, they should do activities that produce long term pleasure. 而在专注时间段 就该做那些产生长期愉悦的活动

This is a good way to balance both short-term and long-term pleasures on a daily basis. 这是一种在日常任务中 平衡短期和长期愉悦的好办法

This one is a variation of the “not to-do” list. 练习四 建立优先清单 这是 不要去做清单 的变体

You write down a list of all the activities you can do in a day and then you prioritize 写下一天中你能做的所有活动 然后按优先级

them on a scale of 0-100. 评分 分数范围为0-100

Activities that receive a score of zero are activities that you shouldn’t do. 评为0分的 就是你不该去做的活动

All activities should add up to a total score of 100. 所有活动的评分 加起来的总和应为100分值

This can help you prioritize your time - like the robot. 这可以帮助你 像机器人一样 优先规划时间

Everyone can determine their own percentages of how to spend their time but I like to follow 每个人可根据自身用时的情况 来决定设定比例 不过我喜欢遵循

an 80/20 rule. 80/20法则

80% of my time should be spent on activities that produce long-term pleasure and 20% of 80%时间应用在制造长期愉悦的项目上 20%用于

it should be spent on activities that produce short-term pleasure. 获得短期愉悦的活动上

Like the “not to-do” list, it’s a good idea to keep your priority list out in front 跟不要去做列表一样 时刻让优先列表放在可见之处

of you. 是一个好方法

It’s also good practice to write a new one each day before you start working. 并且每天在开始工作前 加写一个新活动 也是一个好练习

Just to put it all together and to give you an example of how these tactics can be implemented, 我来举一个如何把这一切运用起来 如何实行这些策略的列子

my day is structured like this: * 80% focused on long term activities like 我的一天构成如下 80%时间集中注意力进行带来长期愉悦的项目

writing, animating, and going to the gym * 20% focused on short term pleasures. 如写作 做动画 健身 20%用于短期愉悦活动

Right now, this mostly consists of reading. 目前主要包括阅读

* I’ll start writing or animating at 1PM and my quitting time is 10PM 我会在下午1点开始写作或做动画 结束时间为晚上10点

* I separate my work time using the standard Pomodoro blocks of 25 minutes, followed by 我用标准番茄工作法规划工作时间 25分钟一轮

a 5 minute break, and a longer 30 minute break after every fourth Pomodoro 休息5分钟 每四个番茄时间后 休息30分钟

In summary, keep yourself optimally pleasured in the short term with enough thought for 总而言之 在长期愉悦项目上有足够专注 同时在短期愉悦活动上 让自身得到

the long term. 最适宜的满足

This is mainly done through the prioritization of activities that will maximize your net 这主要就是通过 优先化各种活动让一生净愉悦值

pleasure in life. 最大化来做到
